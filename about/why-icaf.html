WHY ICAF
A lack of creativity impedes innovation and prosperity, while a lack of mutual empathy endangers peace and democracy.
Problem Statement
Creativity researcher KH Kim found that American creativity declined from the 1990s to 2008, and the slide since 2008 may even be worse. Creativity guru Ken Robinson averred that children get schooled out of their creativity. E. Paul Torrance, widely regarded as the “father” of creativity, discovered the “4th -grade” in children’s creativity. Once this slump gains a foothold, it holds back divergent and critical thinking that propels invention and innovation.

Though creativity is treated as something inherently positive, it is morally neutral. Malevolent or dark creativity intentionally harms the perceived opponents or outgroups. What makes creativity positive for the advancement of the human species and the protection of planet Earth? Empathy. Primatologist Frans de Waal defines empathy as humankind “survival package”. Empathy could well be the mother of all emotions.

Recent neuroscientific findings portray the human brain as a creative powerhousehardwired for empathy because of “mirror neurons”. Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) or the transmission of fear, hatred, or trauma passed from one generation to the next, like physical traits or melanin, can muddy the mirror neurons, making activation necessary.
Why the arts?
In its 2021 report: Art for Life’s Sake: The Case for Arts Education, the American Academy of Arts & Sciences provides “evidence of the attributes, values, and skills that come from arts education, including social and emotional development, improvements in school engagement, as well as vital civic and social participation.” In sum, the arts can change a person. Art programs are also cost effective compared to other interventions. Notably, the concepts of creativity and empathy are both rooted in the arts; hence, the arts can help develop these vital human attributes.
Theory of Change
Founded in 1997 to serve American children as their national arts organization and the world’s children as their global arts organization, the International Child Art Foundation (ICAF) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with Federal Tax ID 52-2032649.

Mission: To cultivate American children’s creativity and grow mutual empathy among them and their peers worldwide as building blocks for a prosperous and peaceful future.

Vision: To democratize creativity for the Fourth Industrial Revolution and mainstream mutual empathy for “a more perfect union”.

ICAF’s programmatic approach is based on the belief that creativity can be learned while empathy can be taught. Its unique pedagogy for children’s creative and empathic development draws upon over twenty-five years of experience working with youth nationwide and worldwide. By employing the arts as a dynamic activity, ICAF helps children reframe their self-identity, first as “artist-athletes” for self-improvement, then as “creative-empaths” for positive social change.
The Arts Olympiad
ICAF’s Arts Olympiad has grown over the years into the world’s largest school art program. The Arts Olympiad inspires students to embrace the “Artist-Athlete Ideal” of the creative mind and healthy body (mente sana in corpo sano). Through structured lesson plans, ICAF awakens the dormant “inner-artists” and the slumbering “inner-athletes” for students to begin thinking of themselves and others as “artist-athletes” — not jocks, nerds, techies, or artsy. This revised self-image solidifies when students limn themselves as artist-athletes in the artwork they produce under the Arts Olympiad.

Blending the artistic and the athletic mindsets nurtures creativity, builds confidence, and boosts resilience, enabling students to overcome fears, anxieties, or adverse childhood experiences (ACEs).

Creativity is a leadership skill for the privileged but a survival skill for the poor because it levels the playing field and can empower disadvantaged students to break the vicious cycle of intergenerational poverty.
World Children’s Festival
ICAF brings together young artist-athletes from across America and nearly 100 participating countries to its festivals, traditionally held at The National Mall across the U.S. Capitol. ICAF choreographs the festival’s workshops, activities, and training to produce a transformative experience that infuses creativity with empathy through co-creation and collaborative mural-making.

Once emboldened as “creative-empaths”, children can disrupt the intergenerational transmission of trauma and hatred to work together with outgroups for a prosperous and peaceful future.
